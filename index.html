Democracy 101

How Elections Work

Six essential phases every citizen should understand

01
Do this first

Voter Registration

Citizens must register before an election using a valid government ID and proof of residence. In India, apply via voters.eci.gov.in or your local BLO. Your EPIC (Voter ID) card is your primary voting credential.

02
Before voting

Candidate Nominations

Political parties or independent candidates file nomination papers within a defined window. The Election Commission of India (ECI) scrutinizes eligibility — including criminal disclosures — before approving the final ballot.

03
Public phase

Campaigning Period

Approved candidates campaign within legally defined periods and spending limits. The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) governs all parties. Campaigning must stop 48 hours before polling day — the "Silent Period."

04
Main event

Voting Day

Bring your EPIC card and booth slip to vote via EVM (Electronic Voting Machine). Booths open 7am–6pm. You may use NOTA — None of the Above — if you find no candidate suitable. Your vote is completely secret.

05
Democratic outcome

Vote Counting & Results

VVPAT slips allow paper-trail verification of EVM results. Votes are counted and tallied under official supervision with party agents present. Results are declared once all counts are certified by the Returning Officer.

06
Democracy complete

Swearing In & Transition

Elected officials take office through an official swearing-in ceremony administered by the President or Governor. The outgoing government ensures a smooth, constitutional handover of power.

From Start to Finish

Election Timeline

A typical election cycle from announcement to sworn-in officials

6–12 months before

Election Announced

Official election date declared by the ECI via a press conference. The Model Code of Conduct comes into immediate effect. Political parties begin candidate selection through their internal processes.

4–6 months before

Voter Registration Opens

Citizens can register or update their voter details via voters.eci.gov.in, the Voter Helpline App, or their local BLO (Booth Level Officer). EPIC cards are issued to new registrants.

3–4 months before

Candidate Nominations

Parties and independents file nomination papers with the Returning Officer. The election body scrutinizes affidavits (criminal records, assets) and approves valid candidates for the final ballot.

2–3 months before

Campaign Period Begins

Candidates actively campaign across constituencies. Model Code of Conduct is strictly enforced — spending limits and conduct rules apply. Campaign finance is audited by Election Commission observers.

48 hours before

Silent Period Begins

All campaigning, advertising and public meetings must stop 48 hours before polls open. Voter slips (booth slip) have been distributed to registered citizens. Check your booth location now.

Election Day

Polls Open Live

Registered voters cast ballots via EVM at designated booths. Booths open 7am to 6pm. Bring your EPIC Voter ID card. VVPAT machines print a slip confirming your vote. NOTA is always an option.

After voting

Results & Swearing In

Votes are counted under party observers' watch, VVPAT results verified, and winner announced by the Returning Officer. New government sworn in by the President / Governor. Democracy complete.

Quick FAQ

Common election questions answered — works even without an AI key

How do I register to vote in India?

You can register online at voters.eci.gov.in or via the Voter Helpline App. You need to be at least 18 years old and an Indian citizen. Required documents: Aadhaar card or any government-issued photo ID, and proof of residence (utility bill, ration card, bank passbook).

After registering, you will receive an EPIC card (Electors Photo Identity Card) — your official Voter ID. You can also check your registration status by calling the Voter Helpline at 1950.

How does an EVM (Electronic Voting Machine) work?

An EVM consists of two units: the Control Unit (operated by the Presiding Officer) and the Ballot Unit (pressed by the voter). The ballot unit lists candidates with symbols and corresponding blue buttons.

When you press a button, a beep confirms your vote and the machine locks — preventing double voting. EVMs are standalone devices with no internet connection, making them tamper-resistant. A VVPAT (Voter Verified Paper Audit Trail) machine prints a paper slip showing your vote, visible through a glass window for 7 seconds as a receipt.

What is NOTA and when should I use it?

NOTA stands for "None of the Above." It appears as the last option on the EVM ballot with a standardized symbol (a ballot paper with a cross). Introduced by the Supreme Court in 2013, it lets voters formally reject all candidates without abstaining from voting.

Important: NOTA votes are counted and published, but they do not affect the outcome — the candidate with the most votes still wins, even if NOTA gets more votes in that constituency. It is a tool for expressing civic dissatisfaction on record.

How do I find my polling booth and constituency?

You can find your polling booth in three ways:

1. Online: Visit electoralsearch.eci.gov.in and enter your name, EPIC number, or Aadhaar details to get your booth address and part number.

2. Booth Slip: The Election Commission distributes a voter slip (booth slip) to every registered voter before election day — it includes your booth address, part number, and serial number in the voter roll.

3. Helpline: Call 1950 (toll-free Voter Helpline) to get your booth details over the phone.

What is the Model Code of Conduct (MCC)?

The Model Code of Conduct is a set of guidelines issued by the Election Commission of India that comes into effect the moment an election is announced. It applies to all political parties, candidates, and the ruling government.

Key rules: No new government schemes or announcements that influence voters. No use of government machinery for campaigns. No hate speech or appeals based on religion/caste. Polling day is a paid holiday. Campaign must stop 48 hours before voting. Violations can be reported to the ECI or via the cVIGIL app with photo/video evidence.
